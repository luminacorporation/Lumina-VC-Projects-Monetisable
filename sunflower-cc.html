Heliotropism

Young sunflowers track the sun across the sky throughout the day.

This east-to-west movement is called heliotropism. As they mature, most sunflowers fix their gaze permanently east — warming bees in the morning!

🌀

Fibonacci Spirals

The seeds inside a sunflower head form perfect mathematical spirals.

The spiral count always follows the Fibonacci sequence — usually 34 one way and 55 the other. Nature is literally doing math in your garden!
Hover to reveal more ✨
📏

Giant Growth

The tallest sunflower ever recorded reached over 9 metres high.

Grown in Germany in 2014, it stood 9.17 m tall. A single sunflower head can contain up to 2,000 seeds!

💛

Symbol of Hope

Sunflowers represent loyalty, adoration, and longevity across cultures.

In Chinese culture they symbolise long life and good luck. In Native American culture they represent harvest and strength. They even help clean soil after nuclear disasters!
Hover to reveal more ✨
🐝

Bee Magnets

One sunflower can attract dozens of bee species, supporting entire ecosystems.

Sunflowers produce abundant nectar and pollen. Their UV-reflecting petals act as landing guides for pollinators — like a runway lit up just for bees!
Hover to reveal more ✨
🌍

World's Oil

Sunflower oil is one of the most widely used cooking oils on Earth.

Ukraine and Russia together produce over 60% of the world's sunflower oil. The seeds are also eaten as snacks, used in bird feed, and even turned into biofuel!
